Marketing Strategy
Step 1: Update Your KDP Keywords

You are already on page one organically at position 12 for “reincarnation books” (14,768 monthly searches on Amazon). The keyword metadata is working. But niche research shows the rest of the top searches for this term are dominated by fiction, manga, and kids’ categories. None of which are your market. Your 7 KDP keyword fields need to work harder to reach non-fiction spiritual readers specifically.

✓
Target non-fiction buyer intent, not genre fiction
The surrounding searches for “reincarnation” are dominated by manga, fiction, and free Kindle variations. These are not your buyer. Your KDP keyword fields should target terms like “life after death non-fiction,” “near death experience book,” and “what happens after death.” These reach readers looking specifically for serious, paid content on this topic.
✓
Use your category placement to expand reach
Your book is currently in Books › Religion & Spirituality › Occult & Paranormal › Psychic Phenomena › Near-Death Experiences. The #1 result in this niche is ranked across three categories simultaneously: New Age › Reincarnation (#77), Out-of-Body Experience (#95), and New Age Mysticism (#99). Requesting a second category placement in New Age › Reincarnation would add a second browse path at no cost.
✓
Use the Readerbull keyword recommendations in this audit
The keywords section below contains the non-fiction relevant terms from this research. Copy them directly into your 7 KDP keyword fields. Do not use all 7 fields on variations of “reincarnation” alone. Spread reach across adjacent buyer-intent searches.
↓
Step 2: Build to 15 Reviews Before Running Ads

With 11 ratings and reviews you are close. Reaching 15 gives your listing the social proof it needs to convert ad traffic into sales. At 15 reviews you are a credible, competitive title in this niche. The more reviews you build from there, the stronger your conversion rate becomes. But 15 is a solid launchpad for your first ad campaign.

✓
Use an ARC service to reach 20 to 30 reviews quickly
BookSirens, Hidden Gems, and NetGalley connect authors with readers who have opted in to receive free books in exchange for honest reviews. These are legitimate, Amazon-compliant services. The spirituality and metaphysics readership on these platforms is large and active. A well-presented ARC campaign for this title could generate 20 to 40 reviews within 4 to 6 weeks.
✓
Add a review request inside the book
Place a natural, warm request mid-book and at the back. Many readers who genuinely enjoyed the book simply never think to leave a review without a prompt. Do not ask for a specific star rating. Amazon's terms prohibit this. A genuine ask yields genuine responses.
✓
Reach near-death experience and reincarnation communities
IANDS (International Association for Near-Death Studies) has an active online community. Reddit’s r/NDE and r/reincarnation have tens of thousands of members. Spirituality and past-life Facebook groups are populated by exactly the readers who would connect with this book and are likely to leave a thoughtful review if asked sincerely.
↓
Step 3: Launch Amazon Ads for the Paperback

This is the action that changes the revenue picture. Every paid paperback in this niche making over $82/month is running ads. The sponsored ads results for “reincarnation books” show that the dominant ad performer is Many Lives, Many Masters (4.7★, 24,981 reviews), but that is not your primary competitor. The realistic ad competitors are paid paperbacks with 53 to 355 reviews, and your $14.99 paperback is well-positioned to compete in that tier once reviews reach 15.

✓
Start with Sponsored Products at $5 to $7/day
Target keyword phrases: “life after death book,” “near death experience book,” and “reincarnation non-fiction.” These reach buyers with paid-book intent rather than browsers looking for free ebooks. Run for 14 days before reviewing results. Do not increase budget until the campaign shows a profitable ACoS.
✓
Add Sponsored Display ads on competitor ASINs
Target the product pages of Children Who Remember Previous Lives (BSR 147,303, $825/mo) and Edgar Cayce on Reincarnation (BSR 172,303, $447/mo). Buyers browsing those pages are already in a paid-book buying mindset for exactly this topic. A well-placed display ad can intercept that intent directly.
✓
Target ACoS below 35% for profitability
At $14.99 paperback, your royalty is approximately $4.84. A profitable ACoS means spending less than $4.84 per sale in ad spend. Pause any keyword without a sale after 14 days. Scale only what converts consistently. The Thread of Theseus benchmark (53 reviews, $259.80/month with ads) is a realistic 60 to 90 day target.

A Second Title Compounds Discoverability
Two books by the same author cross-sell each other automatically. Amazon’s “customers also bought” system means every reader who buys one title is shown the other. Each new title is also a new keyword footprint, a new category placement, and a new set of search rankings.
1
When Children Remember: A Parent’s Guide to Past Lives
A fast-growing search area. Parents actively look for guidance when a child shows apparent past-life memories. Few modern, accessible books address this for a general audience. High emotional purchase motivation and a clearly defined reader. Very little competition at this specific angle.
2
The Science of Reincarnation: Evidence and Case Studies
Targets sceptical readers and those seeking evidence-based non-fiction. This buyer is willing to pay for well-sourced content and is underserved by the current free ebook market, which skews heavily spiritual rather than scientific. Lower free-ebook competition in this angle.
3
Life Between Lives: What Happens in the Interval
The “life between lives” concept has its own dedicated readership, driven by Michael Newton’s foundational work. A modern, accessible title in this sub-niche faces lighter competition than the broad reincarnation category and reaches a highly motivated buyer with strong purchase intent.
